The Role of 3D Imaging and Thermal Imaging

3D imaging allows surgeons to examine wounds in high detail, including depth, shape, and surrounding tissue. It supports accurate planning for reconstructive procedures and helps reduce surgical risks. This technology enables better visualization of injury severity and healing progress over time. Thermal imaging reveals temperature variations in tissue, indicating issues like inflammation or infection. It can also detect poor blood circulation, which is critical for determining tissue viability. Together, 3D and thermal imaging provide a more complete understanding of wound condition. This combination improves diagnostic accuracy and enhances surgical precision significantly.

The Need for Integration: Lessons from the Current Crisis

Gunshot wounds are a major issue in war zones, with many needing reconstructive surgery. We aim to combine 3D scanning, thermal imaging, and ultrasound into one AI-powered tool to:

  • Model wound closure
  • Analyze blood flow and tissue health
  • Improve planning for reconstruction

This solution will help model wound closure and evaluate limb functionality, paving the way for improved plastic surgery outcomes.
